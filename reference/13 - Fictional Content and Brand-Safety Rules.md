# Fictional Content and Brand-Safety Rules

These rules apply to generated filler posts, usernames, organisations, locations,
links, profile data, post images, and future synthetic datasets.

## Core rule

Never generate real product brands, companies, social platforms, hospitals,
public figures, live-looking domains, or real-looking private addresses.

Use generic descriptions or clearly fictional game-world names.

## Required substitutions

| Avoid | Use instead |
|---|---|
| iPhone or named phone model | phone, smartphone, premium smartphone |
| WhatsApp, Facebook, X, TikTok, Reddit | messaging app, social network, social platform |
| Real retailer, bank, courier, hospital | fictional organisation or generic service |
| bit.ly or any live-looking URL | `[link removed]` |
| Real-looking full address | clearly fictional address such as `14 Example Mews` |
| Real public figure or celebrity | fictional person or role |
| Placeholder names such as TargetName | natural fictional handle such as `@MossySkater` |

## Fictional naming

Prefer a reusable fictional setting so posts feel consistent:

- Town: Oakwood
- Health service: Northbridge Health Centre
- Portal: Northbridge Health Portal
- Venue: Lantern Café
- News outlet: Oakwood Chronicle
- Community venue: Oakwood Library
- Handles: short, natural, and clearly invented

Do not repeatedly use words such as `Fictional`, `Example`, `Test`, `TargetName`,
or `PortalName` in visible post text. They are acceptable only where a clearly
non-live placeholder is necessary.

## Links

Generated links must never resolve to a real site.

Preferred visible representation:

```text
[link removed]
```

Do not generate URL shorteners, domains, QR codes, phone numbers, email
addresses, payment handles, or social-media invite links.

## Originality

Generated content must be newly written. Do not reproduce or closely imitate a
real social-media post, identifiable user's writing, branded campaign, slogan,
or copyrighted promotional copy.

## Review requirement

Every generated post requires human review before it enters the Unity-approved
dataset. Reviewers must check:

- policy action and escalation reason;
- realism and internal consistency;
- fictional identities and organisations;
- brand and URL safety;
- difficulty;
- similarity to earlier posts;
- suitability for the intended game day.
