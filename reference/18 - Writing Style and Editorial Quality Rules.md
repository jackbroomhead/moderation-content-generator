# Writing Style and Editorial Quality Rules

## Voice

- Write posts as real people, local organisations, creators, sellers and
  community accounts would write them.
- Vary length, punctuation, tone and vocabulary.
- Most posts should have no hashtags.
- Do not repeatedly end usernames in numbers such as 99 or 92.
- Avoid policy language inside visible post text.
- Use contemporary UK English by default.

## Immersion

Use plausible invented names, handles, venues and organisations. Do not use
visible placeholders such as:

- TargetUser
- RealPlayerName
- PortalName
- FictionalUser
- Example Street
- Test Hospital
- user191982

Real cities and broad public areas may appear. Do not deliberately identify a
real private person or use a real private address.

## Brands and links

Avoid major product brands, named social platforms and exact product models.
Use generic terms such as smartphone, games console, messaging app, payment
account and parcel service.

Use `[link removed]` only when a link is necessary to understand the case.
Approved posts should normally have no link.

## Data safety

Never generate usable phone numbers, emails, card details, bank account
numbers, routing numbers, sort codes or payment handles.

## Policy alignment

- Obvious spam, phishing and one-off targeted insults should normally be Remove.
- Escalate requires ambiguity, credible danger or specialist investigation.
- Misinformation removal requires the post itself to assert or promote the
  harmful falsehood.
- A post debunking misinformation is not itself misinformation.
- Self-Harm / Crisis must actually concern self-harm, suicide risk,
  eating-disorder harm or personal crisis.
- Unrelated medical emergencies are not Self-Harm cases.
- Low-stakes false claims without meaningful public harm may be approved.
- Animal-cruelty cases must be text-only and non-graphic.

## Explanations

Use one or two concise sentences. State the decisive policy fact and avoid
listing unrelated policies.
